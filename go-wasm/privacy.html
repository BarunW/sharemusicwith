Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 June 2026

ShareMusicWith lets you build a small public page of playlist links and share it. This policy explains, in plain language, what we collect, why, and what we don't. By using the site you agree to what's described here.

What you publish is public

When you build and publish a page we store the content you enter — your display name, handle, short bio, page/playlist titles, and the playlist links you add. This content is public: anyone with your link, or who finds it on the Discover page, can see it. Don't put anything private in it.

Publishing also generates a secret edit link for your page. Anyone who has that link can edit your page, so keep it private. We store it only to authorise edits.

Analytics we collect

To rank and surface playlists (Trending, Popular, Today, Fresh) we record an event when a page is viewed or a playlist link is opened or played. For each event we store the page's handle, the event type, the referring URL, and your browser's User-Agent string.

To count each visitor only once per day and to filter out bots, we derive a one-way, salted hash of your IP address. We never store your raw IP address, and the hash can't be reversed back to it.

What we do not collect

  • No accounts, passwords, or email sign-up.
  • No advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.
  • We do not sell your data or share it with advertisers.

Cookies and local storage

We don't use tracking cookies. While you're building a page, an unpublished draft is saved in your browser's local storage, on your device only — it is not sent to us until you publish.

Third-party content

Pages can embed players from services such as Spotify, Apple Music, and YouTube. When those embeds load, the third party may receive your IP address and set their own cookies, governed by their own privacy policies, not this one. We also load web fonts from Google Fonts.

Data retention and your choices

A published page stays up until it is removed. To take your page down or change it, use your edit link, or contact us and we'll remove it. Analytics events are kept in aggregate to power the rankings.

Contact

Questions or removal requests: privacy@sharemusicwith.net.

ShareMusicWith is a project in active development. This is a plain-language summary, not legal advice; depending on where you and your visitors are (e.g. GDPR/CCPA), you may need additional terms — have it reviewed before relying on it.
